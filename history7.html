1932 Siamese Revolution

The Siamese Revolution of 1932 marked the end of centuries of absolute monarchy and introduced a constitutional system. Orchestrated by the People’s Party, this peaceful coup forced King Prajadhipok (Rama VII) to accept a new constitution, ushering in a parliamentary system and modern governance. While this transformation was groundbreaking, it also brought political instability, military coups, and power struggles in the decades that followed. Nevertheless, the revolution was a key milestone in Thailand’s political evolution and its ongoing struggle for democracy.
