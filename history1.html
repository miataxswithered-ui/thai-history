Founding of Sukhothai

The Sukhothai Kingdom is widely regarded as the first unified Thai state, marking the beginning of Thailand’s recorded history. Founded by King Si Intharathit in 1238 after the successful revolt against the Khmer Empire, Sukhothai thrived as a center of Theravada Buddhism, politics, and culture. King Ramkhamhaeng the Great (reigned 1279–1298) is particularly celebrated for introducing the Thai script, fostering a written tradition that helped unify the kingdom’s administration and culture. This era emphasized local governance through the “Father-Governor” system, where the king was seen as a father figure to his subjects, promoting justice and prosperity. Sukhothai’s decline in the 15th century came as Ayutthaya rose to power, absorbing its territories and cultural advancements.
